How Temporary Email Stops Data Brokerage

Data brokers are companies that collect, analyze, and sell your personal information. They buy data from websites, apps, and retailers — and your email address is the central piece of this puzzle. It connects your activity across different platforms, building a detailed profile of your interests, habits, income, health, and political views.

When you use a disposable email, you fragment your digital footprint. The data broker sees a random address with no connection to your real identity. They can't link it to your phone number, physical address, or other accounts. This breaks their ability to build a comprehensive profile of you — protecting your privacy far beyond what any "Do Not Sell My Data" button can offer.

How Temporary Email Works

The technology behind temporary email is surprisingly simple, yet incredibly elegant. When you generate an address through Sarthak Mail, you're creating a unique mailbox that exists only in the cloud, powered by the mail.tm API. Here's the step‑by‑step process:

  1. Domain Reservation: We check the mail.tm service for available domains and select one that's ready to receive emails.
  2. Account Creation: A new account is created with a random username and a secure password. The username always starts with "sarthak" so you know it's yours.
  3. Token Authentication: Your account receives a unique API token. This token is used for all subsequent requests to fetch emails, read messages, and download attachments.
  4. Inbox Ready: Your temporary email address is now active and can receive emails from the entire world. The inbox is accessible only through your browser session.
  5. Email Retrieval: When someone sends you an email, the mail.tm system stores it. Sarthak Mail fetches these messages via the API and displays them in a clean, organized inbox.

The entire process takes less than 3 seconds from clicking "Generate" to having a working email address. And because everything happens on the server side, your temporary email is secure, anonymous, and independent of your device or location.

Best Practices for Using Temporary Mail

  • Use a new address for each service: Never reuse a temporary email. Each sign‑up should get its own unique address so you can track who's selling your data.
  • Keep a copy of important emails: If you receive a verification link or a one‑time password, copy it immediately. Some services may delete emails quickly.
  • Don't use for sensitive communications: Temporary email is not designed for banking, legal documents, or personal correspondence. Use your primary email for those.
  • Generate fresh before each use: If you haven't used Sarthak Mail for a few days, generate a new address. Old ones may have been cleaned from the system.
  • Understand the limitations: You can receive emails, but you cannot send them. This is intentional — it keeps the service simple and secure.
  • Click to copy your address: The purple pill‑shaped address is clickable. One click copies it to your clipboard for easy pasting.

Pro Tip: Use Sarthak Mail to sign up for services that require email verification but that you don't fully trust. It's the safest way to verify your account without exposing your real identity.
